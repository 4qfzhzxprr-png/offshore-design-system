# Materials

The brand's material discipline beyond the palette. Where `VOICE.md` governs how Offshore *sounds*, this file governs how Offshore *feels in the hand*. It's a brand direction — the actual product specs (alloys, GSMs, supplier names) live elsewhere and may evolve. The point of this file is to name the material families and the character each should carry.

## The rule

**Patina is the proof.**

Brass patinates. Bronze develops verdigris. Recycled marine steel oxidizes at the edges. Sailcloth fades. Wax canvas softens. These aren't failure modes — they're how the brand demonstrates contact with the water it's named for. Pristine, untouched-looking materials read as ornamental and disconnected from the mission. Aged materials read as worn-in and earned.

The rule applies to physical goods (watches, straps, apparel, packaging) and — more subtly — to paperwork: foil stamps with slight registration drift, paper that yellows, ink that softens into uncoated stock over time. Don't engineer production to fight aging. Specify materials that age *well*.

The rule's counter: lume on the dial, sapphire crystal on the case, recycled-nylon hardware. These materials *don't* patina — they earn their place by being functionally non-negotiable (legibility in low light, scratch resistance, ocean-recovered provenance). They're the technical materials. Everything else is a patina material.

## Watch case

The case is the brand's largest physical surface and the most direct test of the rule.

**Primary: aluminum-bronze or marine-grade brass.** Develops a green-yellow patina over months of wear, faster with saltwater exposure. The patina is uneven, which is the point — every owner ends up with a watch that looks subtly different from the next. Polish on edges, brushed on flats; no PVD coating.

**Secondary: recycled marine steel** (316L from recovered fishing gear, or equivalent). For the steel-line variant. Edge-oxidation over a year of saltwater exposure is acceptable and visible. No coating.

**Avoid:** polished gold, rose gold, titanium-coated, ceramic. These don't patina. They read as luxury rather than instrument.

## Strap

The strap is the daily-wear surface and where the apparel and watch worlds meet.

**Primary: sailcloth NATO**, ideally recycled from decommissioned sails, in `--navy` or `--sand`. Fades unevenly with sun and saltwater — same patina rule. Brass hardware.

**Secondary: tumbled vegetable-tanned leather**, in `--navy` or a warm cognac that reads as oxidized brass. Develops a darker patina at the wrist contact points. No glossy finish. Stitching in `--bone` thread.

**Avoid:** rubber, silicone, polished-steel mesh, "tropic"-style perforated rubber. These are dive-watch standards but read as athletic / utilitarian. The brand is closer to marine documentary than sports watch.

## Dial and crystal

Where the brand has to be a precision instrument, not a patina object.

**Dial: matte lacquer in `--bone` or `--navy-deep`.** Subtle (sub-millimeter) chapter ring marks in `--brass`. Hour numerals either applied (lume-filled, see below) or printed flat — never beveled or 3D.

**Lume: Super-LumiNova C3 or BGW9.** C3 is more legible green-glow; BGW9 is more atmospheric blue-glow (reads as deep-water bioluminescence — on-brand). Pick one per line, don't mix.

**Crystal: sapphire, flat, AR coating on the underside only.** No domed crystal (reads vintage / luxury), no mineral glass (scratches), no box crystal.

**Hands: brass, polished outer / brushed inner, lume-filled.** Match the case material.

## Packaging

The unboxing surface — where the brand introduces itself by feel.

**Outer box: kraft** (in `--sand` tone or natural unbleached), **unprinted or single foil-stamp.** Foil is `--brass` on kraft; mark + wordmark only, no marketing copy on the box exterior. Construction: hinge-lid, magnet close, no plastic clamshell.

**Box interior liner: bone-tone cardstock, foil-stamped with the depth-contours pattern** (`patterns/depth-contours.svg`) **in `--brass`.** The pattern fills the visible inner-lid surface when opened. This is the moment the brand visually meets the customer.

**Watch cushion: sailcloth-wrapped** (matches strap material), **or undyed canvas.** No silk, no plastic-film tray, no foam insert.

**Insert card: bone uncoated cardstock, navy printed type** (DM Sans 9pt body, Cormorant Garamond display) **+ a single brass foil stamp of the mark.** Card contents: the cause partner for that capsule, the 5% routing, the watch's serial, the date of assembly. No marketing copy — see `VOICE.md` for what to say and how.

## Print and paperwork

The system's smallest physical surface and the most often produced.

**Letterhead and invoice: bone uncoated 100 GSM.** Type per the design system. Mark in `--navy` printed (not foiled — foil on every invoice is precious). Footer: depth-contours pattern in `--brass-deep` at 40–60px height, navy hairline above it.

**Business cards: bone uncoated 350 GSM.** Mark + name + email and phone in `--navy`. Reverse: single `--brass` foil-stamped mark, centered, blind. No QR codes.

**Hangtags (apparel): kraft 300 GSM, single-color screen-print in `--navy`.** Mark + cause partner on front, care instructions on reverse. String: natural unbleached cotton.

**Stickers and decals: matte vinyl, two options.** `--navy` mark on `--bone` substrate, or `--bone` mark on `--navy` substrate. No gradient, no glow, no holographic.

**Foils for all packaging: brass (matching `--brass` `#A78546`) only.** Avoid gold-mirror foils (read as luxury), copper foils (too warm), silver and holographic foils (too cold or too cheap).

**Finishes:** uncoated is the default. Spot-UV is reserved for one moment per surface — typically the mark on a presentation card. Blind deboss (no ink, no foil — pure impression) is allowed for the mark on business-card reverse. No matte lamination, no soft-touch coating.

## Apparel

The brand's secondary product line, deployed alongside watches.

**Tee shirts: 100% organic ringspun cotton, 5–6 oz weight, in `--navy`, `--bone`, or `--sand`.** Garment-dyed for softer hand and slight fade variability. Single-needle hem. Reverse-print or front-chest-only — no allover prints. Print in single color: `--brass` on `--navy`, `--navy` on `--bone`, `--navy` on `--sand`.

**Hats: unstructured 6-panel canvas cap, `--navy` or `--sand`.** Front: embroidered C-in-O mark in `--brass` thread. No snapback, no foam-front trucker.

**Outerwear (jackets, vests): wax canvas in `--navy-deep`, brass hardware.** Single embroidered mark on chest. No printed logos.

**Avoid:** hoodies as merch-default (says nothing), polyester, white garments, neon, distressed-by-design "vintage" treatments. The brand's patina rule is about earned aging, not factory-faked aging.

## What to avoid (overall)

- **Polished gold, rose gold, mirror-chrome.** Reads as luxury. The brand is instrument, not jewelry.
- **High-gloss or clear-coated finishes** on any surface. They engineer patina-resistance, which fights the rule.
- **Plastic in any product-facing position.** Use plastic only where it's invisible (internal movement components, packaging structure hidden inside the kraft outer).
- **Holographic, iridescent, or pearlescent finishes.** Read as cheap or trendy.
- **The mark on every surface.** It earns its place by appearing once, well — not by tiling.
- **"Premium," "luxury," "crafted," or "artisanal" as material descriptors.** Specify the material plainly. See `VOICE.md`.
- **Distressed-by-design materials** (pre-frayed, factory-faded, "vintage wash"). The brand earns its patina honestly.
